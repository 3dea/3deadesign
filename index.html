My Story

I completed my Industrial Design Degree at the University of Johannesburg in 2010. Afterwhich, I was offered a position at Barrows Design and Manufacturing having done a 3 week internship with them. I was responsible for ideation of concepts, running brainstorm sessions, sketching out initial concepts and translating those into photorealistic renders. In 2016 I emmigrated to the UK where I continued to work for Barrows London on a freelance capacity. The work environment differed in that I was now in a smaller, more personal team and the projects reflected that. The translation from design to manufacture had become more of my responsibility. In 2019 I took a position at Frontline Display International where I initially joined a team in the Development Design Department of which I later headed up myself with a small team. I am responsible for creating the engineering drawings for which the design concept can be manufactured. Having a design background, that translation is a lot smoother.
